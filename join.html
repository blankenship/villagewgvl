Join The Village Association

Everything you need to know to get involved

The Village Association is building on a historical legacy that supports our identity and positions us to be an economic hub for Greenville. We are a collection of locally-owned businesses and non-profits with a big footprint in the arts and are dedicated to growing and achieving success together. We invite you to join The Village Association.

Mission Statement: This Business Association fosters a diverse and vibrant business district, honors the area's distinctive history, invites thoughtful growth, and encourages a thriving community through engagement with its small business owners, entrepreneurs, artists, creatives, restaurateurs, non-profits, and neighborhood residents.

Before you sign up, please look over the Membership Tiers, Association Committee opportunities, and read the Association’s bylaws. It takes a Village!

What are the advantages of becoming a member?

Advocacy
The Village Association represents the economic vitality and interests in the Village of West Greenville to the City and County. Speaking for all business members with one voice strengthens our ability to seek funding and improvements in The Village.
Credibility
Village Association memberships bring credibility to your organization/business. Membership can enhance a positive perception among consumers and business owners when you’re identified as a member of The Village Association.
Knowledge
Connects each member with a community of professionals and business owners who desire to see your business grow and flourish.
Network & Collaboration
People do business with people they know. The association meetings are an opportunity to meet other business owners and leaders, and potentially grow your business or organization by cultivating and maintaining valuable relationships.
Referrals
Through our website and social media presence, we do our best to connect potential customers with association members that can provide the service or product requested. A personal referral always goes a long way!
Visibility
Being a member allows your business to be found by people looking for your goods or services, whether they are looking online or on foot. Eventually, this may range from inclusion in member directories to grand opening and ribbon cutting ceremonies with elected officials.

Village Association Committees

Promotion Committee
Promotion Committee is responsible for marketing The Village Association of West Greenville. This committee is responsible for creating networking opportunities, organizing events and public relations, posting on social media, and creating and managing The Village website. Creating and overseeing the overall brand of The Village including signage, wayfinding, and marketing and membership materials. It may become necessary for any and all committees to have a point of contact added to the promotion committee when the situation arises. (Membership Committee will be involved in creation and approval of the membership benefits along with Promotion Committee).
City and County Relations Committee
City and County Relations Committee is responsible for documenting the concerns of The Village and making the city and/or county aware of issues, including but not limited to traffic and safety. This committee will follow up on any tasks that the city or county has agreed to look into, or have stated they are scheduling work to be done. This Committee will continue to stay in touch/network with City, County, SCDOT, Sheriff’s and Fire Departments, asking if there are any possible developments The Village needs to be aware of. If news is confirmed, the Village asks that the developer, City/County traffic engineers, or economic development present possible plans to The Village Association.
Public Art & Beautification Committee
Public Art & Beautification Committee is responsible for organizing and assisting in executing any beautification projects that The Village board and Membership approve. The Village ideas on public art will be overseen by this committee and communicated to the board, and to the city or county if necessary. Collaborating and identifying where public art should be placed. Communicate with the city staff liaisons in regards to art maintenance, plant maintenance, watering, and communicate any issues that may arise.
Membership Committee
This committee is the connector of the Association. Membership Committee will be involved with promotion committee to create the membership benefits as well as continue to review the benefits for changes and/or additional benefits. Membership Committee is responsible for recruitment and retention of members, welcoming them to The Village, providing materials about the Village Association, and its community of businesses who work together for a thriving business district. The Committee tasks include membership sales, list of current members, list of possible members, communicating Village business/benefits to members and non-members (when necessary), and confirming the new member is placed on the website. The Membership Committee should also be an educational resource for businesses, an outlet that shows support and direction on navigating the community and doing business here.

Bylaws for The Village Association of West Greenville

(Date 06/17/2024)

Article I – Name and Purpose

Sec. 1 Name. This organization shall be known as The Village Association of West Greenville, hereinafter referred to as “The Village”, “Association”, or “Business Association”. Address: 1211 Pendleton Street, Suite 102, Greenville, SC 29611.

For marketing and branding, the Association names can also include: The Arts District, Arts District of West Greenville, Greenville Arts District, West Greenville Arts District, Village of West Greenville, The Village of West Greenville Arts District, or Village Business Association. No other names are to be used.

Mission Statement: This Business Association fosters a diverse and vibrant business district, honors the area's distinctive history, invites thoughtful growth, and encourages a thriving community through engagement with its small business owners, entrepreneurs, artists, creatives, restaurateurs, non-profits, and neighborhood residents.

Sec. 2 Purpose. The Village Association of West Greenville is a non-profit organization that seeks to support the economic vitality of The Village business area and the surrounding community through informed involvement in matters affecting the quality of life in the area; to recognize and preserve the history of The Village and community area as it relates to the City of Greenville, South Carolina; and to provide a voice for business, non-profit, and resident members of the Association in the commercial development of The Village and surrounding areas. The objectives of The Village include but are not limited to the representation of the interests of the Village business community and service as its primary organization for collaborative marketing and promotional efforts. The Association requests that any and all, including but not limited to, the City of Greenville, developers, researchers, engineers, and interested entities give their due diligence in approaching the Association Membership [Including a Brandon Community Association Representative, and a West Greenville Neighborhood Association Representative] with full or possible intent. These parties are invited to contact the Village Board of Directors to communicate intent and if necessary present or speak at Board or Membership meetings, or both if needed, as it pertains to the Village Association’s Purpose.

Article II – Membership

Sec. 1 Eligibility. Any business owner, partner, corporate officer, or designated representative of any recognized business, business activity, profession, nonprofit, or other legitimate business enterprise located in the Village business district and surrounding areas shall be eligible for membership in the Village Association. Including individuals living in the area and working from home with a legitimate business or service. Businesses outside the community can join without voting rights and for networking and social purposes only. This is also in place for those who may want to attend meetings before deciding to move their business here, and expressing an interest in joining within a year.

Resident Members – Resident members are considered the general community inside and immediately outside the association borders, or those who are affected by business in the Village. This membership means that they subscribe to get Village news and updates and have opportunity to donate.

Sec. 2 Dues. The annual dues of this association for regular members shall be in the amount recommended by the Board and approved by the membership of the Association as part of the approval of the budget at the first meeting of each calendar year.

Sec. 3 Fiscal year. The Fiscal year-end for the organization will be December 31st.

Sec. 4 Association Meetings. Meetings of the full membership of the Association will be held quarterly. Other community members may be asked by the Board to attend specific meetings when outside expertise is needed. Additional Association meetings may be called by the President. Members wishing to make presentations to the Board must request to the President or representative of the Board two weeks or more prior to the next upcoming meeting. Written notice must also be provided on the content of the presentation two weeks prior to the connected meeting. The President or Vice President will decide whether the content is relevant for a board meeting or a full association meeting. Any member of the Board or Membership can request an emergency meeting if the importance of the information grants a formal meeting. The member must communicate the importance to the Board so that the Board can accept or deny the importance and call the meeting.

Article III – Board of Directors or the Board

Sec. 1 General Powers. The management, direction, and control of The Village Association of West Greenville shall be in the “Board of Directors”, who shall be expected to attend Regular and Special meetings of the Board of Directors and Membership meetings of the Association. The Board of Directors shall have and exercise general control of the business and affairs of the Association and shall have the power to act as a deciding body for the Association between meetings. A City Liaison does not have voting power or the ability to speak on behalf of the Association, unless the Board gives approval. A business or marketing consultant of the Association does not have voting power or the ability to speak on behalf of the Association, unless the full Board of Directors gives him or her permission to communicate on their behalf.

Sec. 2 Number, Election, & Tenure. There shall be a minimum of 10 Directors with full voting rights including At Large Members. There should be at least one At Large Member. Board Members will serve a two-year term, unless they request termination on their own accord, and they are not limited in the number of terms they may serve. The Board Members must be members of the Association, and if membership terminates, their directorship terminates. The Board Members will be nominated by membership by November of each Odd Year and Voted on by the Board each December of every Odd Year.

Sec. 3 Regular Board Meetings. Regular meetings of The Board of Directors will be held twice a month and/or a minimum of once per month.

Sec. 4 Special Board Meetings. Special meetings of the Board of Directors may be called by the President at any time by giving at least two-day notice to The Board, and shall specify the particular business to be translated at said meeting.

Sec. 5 Quorum. The quorum for all Board meetings shall be at least 6 members (more than 50%) of the Board of Directors. There must be a quorum present to vote.

If a Board Member has done their homework and is ready, virtually or by phone, to meet they are allowed to vote by proxy.

Sec. 7 Vacancies. Any vacancies that may occur may be filled by a majority vote of the Board for the unexpired term of the Board member whose seat is presently vacant.

Sec. 8 Removal. Any Director may be removed from office for cause deemed sufficient by a majority vote of the Board of Directors at a meeting so called for that purpose and his/her successor may be elected at that meeting.

Examples: Purposeful fraudulent activity with association funds. Speaking publicly or to the media on behalf of the entire Association without approval by the board, and in certain cases the approval of the entire business membership. These are examples only and the board has the right to define when cause is necessary.

Sec. 9 Installation and Term of Office. The notice and introduction of the directors shall take place at the first annual Association meeting and they shall hold office for a 2-year term, or until successor(s) duly elected.

Sec. 10 Duties of the Directors. The Directors of The Village shall have such duties, responsibilities, and powers as are usually associated with their office, except as modified by the Board of Directors, and shall have such additional duties, responsibilities, and powers as may from time to time be conferred upon them by the Board of Directors. The following duties are set forth as a guideline for each Director. The duties, responsibilities, and powers may be altered and/or expanded from time to time, at the discretion of the Board of Directors. Each Director has the authority to select Association members for specific tasks or projects.

Chair/President. The chair/president shall be the Chief Administrative Officer of the Village Association. He or she shall preside over all meetings of this Association and at all meetings of the Board of Directors or Board. The President/s is responsible for planning and scheduling each meeting. The President shall in general supervise and control the day-to-day business affairs of the Association. The President shall carry into effect or cause to be carried into effect all orders or resolutions of the Board of Directors, and shall discharge such other duties as may from time to time be prescribed by the Board of Directors and the Membership.

President-Elect. The President-elect acts in the absence of the President as chairperson of the Board of Directors, works closely with the President to ensure continuity of leadership, supports the efforts of the President, and learns the duties of the President's office. Represents the President at meetings or functions when the President is unable to attend.

Past President. The Past President will act as a liaison to the new Board to ensure a seamless transition. The Past President will advise the new President on continuation of projects that need to be completed. Represent the President at Village functions when the President is unable to attend or as needed. Assist in developing goals and membership growth.

Treasurer. The Treasurer shall be responsible for the receipt, care, and custody of all funds of the Association. He or she shall maintain safe custody of all Association funds and securities. All funds of the Association will be deposited to the credit of the Village Association of West Greenville in depositories designated by the Board of Directors and will be dispensed and reported to the Board by the Treasurer or the President. The Treasurer shall make disbursements based on approved budgets and Board votes. The Treasurer shall keep accurate and complete financial books, which shall be open for inspection at any time. The Treasurer shall furnish a fiscal year-end financial report to all Members.

Secretary. The Secretary shall attend all meetings of the Association and Board of Directors and shall be responsible for recording the minutes of all proceedings in books belonging to the Association. The Secretary shall be responsible for notifying all Directors of scheduled and called Board meetings and shall be responsible for attending to all correspondence of The Village Association. The Secretary may be assigned duties and responsibilities by the President or Board of Directors.

At Large Members. Board of Directors Members with voting rights. These members represent the interests of the membership. This person/s must be able to attend monthly Board of Directors meetings and help make decisions on behalf of the Association of West Greenville’s members. The President can give At Large Members a project or task when and if the event arises. At Large Members must be able to step into a role if others on the Board of Directors are not available.

Community Representative. Considered a Board Member with voting rights, yet cannot step into a role when needed unless this person is also a business member of the Association. This can also be revoked if the Board relies on this position/person already on a current role they have taken on. This person brings perspective to the board in how the Village Association ideas and decisions will affect the community and bring positive ideas for community involvement.

Communications Representative. Considered a voting member of the Board of Directors. This representative will be responsible for communicating to the full membership with community and/or membership news that the Board needs release by email or marketing. This role will communicate and oversee the progress of the marketing, social media, and events committees.

Artist Representative. Considered a voting member of the Board of Directors. This representative will be responsible for collaborating on each endeavor the Village undertakes to make sure that there are opportunities for artists to be involved in the Village Arts District. This role includes being educated upfront on any and all Village events and reviewing Village material in production. This person will hold a gathering of all the artists in the village for communal meetings to discuss a marketing routine that is resourceful and beneficial to all Village artists.

Auxiliary Buildings Representative. This Board Member will make sure all members in buildings not located directly on Pendleton Street’s historic commercial corridor area are properly represented in ideas brought to the table when discussing events, maps, marketing, and future endeavors. The Buildings currently include: Poe West, Flatiron, Railside Studios, Art Bomb, The Center for Creative Arts, Woodside Mill, Brandon Mill, Bank Building (future Woven), 547 Perry, Artistry Workshop & Gallery, Venture X, West Greenville Plaza, Center for Educational Equality, and Boxing Club.

City Staff Liaison. A City Staff Liaison is not a voting member. This person will attend board and regular meetings as a representative of the City of Greenville. This person is able to answer city related questions and/or offer to follow up with correct information. This person would have quick access on how to join send to new businesses they know that are coming to the area. Also, this liaison will communicate to the Board if they know a new development is coming.

COMMITTEES - Committees and/or Sub Committees can be created. The addition of Project Chairpersons can be deemed necessary. Committee or Sub Committee Chairs will send an email to the Board of Directors before a board meeting to update on specific project progress.

Sec. 11 Liability of The Village of West Greenville/ Board of Directors/ Members.

The Village of West Greenville is a voluntary membership association whose purpose is to support the economic vitality, history, and commercial development future of Greenville’s West Village community as stated in Article I, Sec. 2. No Director/Officer/Member shall be held liable for any acts or omissions related to the Association unless the Director/Officer/Member has personally committed fraudulent or criminal acts with intent.

Article IV – Public Welfare.

Sec. 1 The Association is organized to operate primarily in promoting the common and general welfare of the community and business area under Section 501 (c) (4) of the Internal Revenue Code, or corresponding section of any future federal tax code. This includes but is not limited to operating for charitable, educational, historical, research, and scientific purposes.

Sec. 2 No part of the net earnings of the Association shall inure to the benefit of, or be distributable to its members, trustees, directors, officers, or other private persons or business entities, except that the Association shall be authorized and empowered to pay reasonable compensation for services rendered and to make payments and distributions in furtherance of the purposes outlined in Article IV, Sec. 1 above. No substantial part of the activities of the Association shall be the carrying on of propaganda, or otherwise attempting to influence legislation, and the Association shall not participate in, or intervene in (including the publishing or distribution of statements) any political campaign on behalf of or in opposition to any candidate for public office. Notwithstanding any other provision of these articles, the Association shall not carry on any other activities not permitted to be carried on (a) by a corporation exempt from federal income tax under section 501 (c) (4) of the Internal Revenue Code, or the corresponding section of any future federal tax code, or (b) by a corporation, contributions to which are deductible under section 170 (c) (2) of the Internal Revenue Code, or the corresponding section of any future federal tax code.

Sec. 3 Upon the dissolution of the corporation, assets shall be distributed for one or more exempt purposes within the meaning of section 501(c)(4) of the Internal Revenue Code, or the corresponding section of any future federal tax code, or shall be distributed to the federal government, or to a state or local government, for a public purpose. Any such assets not so disposed of shall be disposed of by a Court of Competent Jurisdiction of Greenville County, South Carolina, exclusively for such purposes or to such organization or organizations, as said Court shall determine, which are organized and operated exclusively for such purposes.
